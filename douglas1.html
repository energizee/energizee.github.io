Intro

- Question: What is social media and how does it relate to sustainable cities and communities?
- Answer: Social media is a way of communicating with others virtually, which introduces the idea of variety and adaptation to differing environments.
In the current day and age, it is easy to gain access to an electronic device and be exposed to the internet. Social media is unavoidable nowadays (especially if you live in a developing country) due to people being closer with their electronic devices. While some may view this to be negatively affecting society, with the help of computation it is not necessarily a pathogen that is plaguing society as we speak.

Advantages:

Social media can stimulate community learning and overall enhance the production of communities. Due to the uniqueness of everyone in a community, it can often be seen as a wonderful way of gaining completely new perspectives that an individual may have not had isolated. (1) “You can use social media to research, learn, and share best practices, lessons learned, and innovative solutions from other communities or experts.” This will often build stronger communities with individual or shared perspectives; this can be weaponised in hopes of creating a brighter future, with more inspiration and ease of access to knowledge.
Some may opt to use social media in a way of reaching out to others in hopes of making new companions, contacting loved ones, learning diverse cultures etc. Humans were not designed to live in isolation and social media is more than capable of closing that gap with ease. It is not recommended to base your entire persona around social media due to it potentially having negative effects on individuals, despite that it is effective at connecting people if used safely and correctly.

Disadvantages:

As mentioned before, social media is a fantastic way of exposing yourself to the unknown, but you’d be none the wiser to undergo overexposure as: (2) “Many studies reveal that being online for a long period of time in an empty space can create a feeling of loneliness since the person’s inner mind gradually develop solitude by over exposure to others happy moments.” While developed countries and akin may be fortunate enough to have access to such a powerful tool, said tool can also result in great hardships if said person is not careful. Therefore, it is recommended to limit yourself to the amount of content you consume on social media in hopes of preventing an event like this.

While social media can be a seen as a fantastic way of growing and forming stable communities it can also be disruptive and even be seen as main leading factor to cyberbullying. Many teenagers often find themselves missing sleep due to their free usage of social media. While it may not necessarily be fault of social media, it can depict it as being very obsessive and disruptive due to this phenomenon being a common occurrence. Cyberbullying is not something that would often affect the adult world, depending on how formal the setting is of course, but it is common within teenager world. Internet trolls will often demotivate people or cause unnecessary conflicts and this can be seen as a disadvantage due to social media giving people the power to stay anonymous, allowing for them to be more brutal and harsher.
