Intro

Making a city inclusive, safe, sustainable and resilient highly depends on the jobs of the cities inhabitants. As cities grow more reliant on computer science every day, the demand for software developers will skyrocket in every industry. Computer science will lead societies to a cleaner, safer and much more efficient future. This will be executed in a variety of ways including assisting people in their jobs, making jobs more eco-friendly and producing more job opportunities.

We are now in a time where most office based companies make use a home and office hybrid work system which heavily depends on software and information systems which are easy to access and simple to use. This is highlighted in an investigation conducted by the Office for National Statistics which explains that between February 2022 and May 2022 the percentage of hybrid workers increased from 13% to 24% showing a continuous growth in the popularity of hybrid working. (1) This reinforces the point that there is an ever-growing demand for computer scientists to to constantly update and create improved software to allow people to communicate, work and ultimately continue to grow in their own field. In addition to hybrid working, the use of software has been utilised in more niche industries including engineering, data analysis, construction and sales management. Each industry which incorporates computer science into its business will work far more efficiently leading to a more significant contribution to society, in turn, either making society safer, more sustainable or generating more wealth for that city.

As mentioned previously, the construction industry has greatly benefited from the use of software in the workplace. Construction has come a long way from being a strenuous, frustrating and time consuming job. With todays rate of population increase, cities cannot wait for months and months for architects and builders to use old fashioned methods to create homes for people- they need something simple and efficient. Thanks to computer science, nowadays they have extremely powerful Computer Aided Design (CAD) software that allows for sketched to be created quickly and shared instantly significantly reducing production time. In addition, new buildings are much safer thanks to the testing that can be conducted on building models in a virtual environment. “In developing countries, poor quality construction has lead to spontaneous building collapse and, during earthquakes, to major disaster”(2) Therefor this greatly benefits developing countries that may be frequent victims of natural disasters as buildings designed through this software are far less likely to collapse and be hazardous to those within its vicinity.

Allowing computer science to change healthcare systems will greatly benefit a city. “On average across these OECD countries, the median waiting times for more minor surgery like cataract operation was 95 days in 2018, and longer for more major surgery like hip replacement (110 days) and knee replacement (140 days).” (3) With most current healthcare systems on their knees it is crucial that we allow computer science to make changes. Creating new databases and information storage systems will lead to shorter waiting times, better accessibility for the less fortunate and overall better safety for all. With the use of robotically assisted surgeries, diagnostics and treatments, healthcare workers who are overrun with work will be able to work more efficiently and with much less stress. This results in a better workplace environment, making a job in healthcare far more appealing to young people which further reduces staff shortages and stress on a city’s healthcare system.
